- **Governance and efficiency risk:** Without strong institutional oversight, nationalized entities may face productivity declines or misallocation of capital.  
- **Global competitiveness risk:** Overextension of state ownership could reduce foreign investment appeal and slow innovation.  
- **Policy inertia risk:** Once nationalized, reversing or reforming ownership structures may become politically difficult, reducing flexibility in downturns.

### **3. Final Confidence Level**
**Confidence: 78%**  
The macroeconomic fundamentals support a cautiously optimistic outlook for partial nationalization, but execution quality and institutional capacity remain decisive.

### **4. What Would Change My Mind**
- **Deterioration in fiscal or external balances** (e.g., rising debt-to-GDP or capital flight).  
- **Evidence of declining productivity or innovation** in state-led sectors.  
- **Political instability or governance erosion** undermining policy coherence.  
- **Emergence of superior private‑public partnership models** demonstrating better efficiency and social outcomes.

Proceed with **selective nationalization under strong fiscal discipline and governance safeguards**, integrating macro stability with long-term strategic control in key sectors.
